# Hero

## Usage
### Use when
- Grabbing the user's attention.
- Introducing the content of a page in an aesthetically pleasing way.
- Displaying the title of the screen.
### Don't use when
- Displaying big chunks of text.

## Anatomy
- **Background image**: the visual the hero is built on.
- **Title**: a short, attention-grabbing heading.
- **Flow illustration** (optional): an NS branding element in white or blue.
- **Additional content** (optional): supporting elements in fuller combinations.

## Configurations
### Text color
Light or dark, chosen for contrast against the image.

### Flow illustration
Available in white and blue variants.

### Content alignment
Aligns to the top or bottom of the hero.

## Placement
- Always place the hero at the top of the screen; it spans the full width of the viewport.

## Behavior
- Minimum height is 360px; the hero scales automatically as its content grows.
- The Flow illustration doesn't scale and sticks to the top or bottom as the height changes.
- When content scrolls under the [Top bar](https://design.ns.nl/4a05a30ad/p/37561d-top-bar), the top bar transitions to its Default type.

## Best practices
- Use a clear, concise title.
- Ensure high contrast between the text and the background image — dark text on a light image, white text on a dark image.

## Content guidelines
- Keep titles short.

## Accessibility
- Ensure enough contrast between the text and the background image. `[1.4.3]`
